**Design Movement:** Prairie Modern Real Estate Editorial. This approach draws from Frank Lloyd Wright-inspired horizontal architecture, regional materiality, and contemporary editorial real estate brochures.

**Core Principles:** The interface should feel grounded, trustworthy, and locally competent through long horizontal lines, layered planes, and generous breathing room. Content hierarchy should favor decisive homeowner outcomes over decorative flourishes. Every visual element should suggest structure, stability, and practical guidance. Conversion components should feel like concierge intake rather than aggressive lead traps.

**Color Philosophy:** The palette should use deep institutional navy as the trust anchor, limestone and warm paper tones as residential warmth, muted sage as a restoration cue, and restrained gold as a signal of equity and premium service. The emotional intent is calm authority: homeowners should feel that JNK can handle complexity without pressure.

**Layout Paradigm:** Use asymmetric horizontal bands and offset content planes rather than a centered grid. The hero should pair a strong left-side editorial headline with a right-side property image and a floating intake card that crosses the boundary between content and image. Subsequent sections should alternate between full-width narrative strips, staggered service cards, and anchored proof modules.

**Signature Elements:** Subtle roofline angles, blueprint-line borders, and before/after restoration frames should recur throughout the page. Form cards should use layered shadows and a soft paper texture to feel tangible. Service cards should include small architectural icon marks.

**Interaction Philosophy:** Interactions should feel reassuring and controlled. Hover states should gently lift cards, underline CTAs with blueprint-like strokes, and reveal concise explanations without surprising motion.

**Animation:** Use slow fade-up entrances, horizontal wipe reveals that echo architectural drafting, and understated parallax on hero imagery. Avoid bouncy motion; use 500–800ms easing with natural deceleration.

**Typography System:** Use Fraunces or Cormorant Garamond for high-confidence display headings, paired with Source Sans 3 or IBM Plex Sans for forms and body text. Headings should be large, line-height tight, and sentence case. Body copy should use comfortable line lengths and medium weights for reassurance.

## Chosen Direction

The selected design philosophy is **Prairie Modern Real Estate Editorial**. It is the strongest fit because JNK needs to look trustworthy, local, professional, and differentiated from generic cash-buyer landing pages. The rebuild will use deep navy, warm limestone, muted sage, and restrained gold; asymmetric horizontal bands; roofline geometry; blueprint-inspired borders; refined real estate editorial typography; and calm, controlled interactions that reinforce confidence rather than urgency alone.

When making implementation choices, the guiding question is: **Does this choice reinforce or dilute our Prairie Modern Real Estate Editorial philosophy?**
